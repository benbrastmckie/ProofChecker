## Critical Context

### Mathematical Obstruction (from research-043)

When M' is reflexive (CanonicalR M' M'):
- Non-strict density gives W with CanonicalR M W and CanonicalR W M'
- If W ~ M' (mutual accessibility), then [W] = [M'] in the quotient
- W is NOT strictly between M and M' in the quotient order
- Must ESCAPE the reflexive cluster via seriality: F(neg bot) in M' gives a strict future

### Key Lemmas to Use

- `mcs_has_strict_future`: Seriality gives successor MCS
- `irreflexive_mcs_has_strict_future`: If M NOT reflexive, seriality witness is STRICT
- `canonicalR_transitive`: Compose accessibility relations
- `density_frame_condition`: Non-strict version (already proven)

### Proof Structure for seriality_escape

When M' is reflexive:
1. F(neg bot) in M' by seriality axiom
2. Apply canonical_forward_F to get W with CanonicalR M' W and neg(bot) in W
3. neg(bot) = top, so this is just any successor

The question: how to prove W is STRICT (¬CanonicalR W M')?

If W ~ M' (mutual accessibility), then W is also reflexive. But the proof needs to show that SOME successor from a reflexive MCS is strict.

Key insight: Every MCS has G-formulas. If M' is reflexive, GContent(M') subseteq M'. The forward witnesses constructed via Lindenbaum have different G-formulas based on their seed.
