### Key Blocker: F/P Witness Construction

The central completeness blocker is **F/P existential witness construction**:

1. **Truth lemma is bidirectional**: Imp forward case requires backward IH (`ih_psi fam hfam t).mpr`)
2. **Lindenbaum can kill witnesses**: Extension can add G(neg φ) even when F(φ) is present
3. **Task 70 finding**: Separate-direction witnesses (forward_G, backward_H) are sorry-free, but forward_F/backward_P require unbounded nesting resolution

## User Goal Analysis: Strict Temporal Operators

### Proposed Extension: G'/H' (Strict Operators)

| Operator | Proposed Semantics | Relationship to Reflexive |
|----------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| G' | ∀s > t, φ(s) | G ↔ (φ ∧ G') |
| H' | ∀s < t, φ(s) | H ↔ (φ ∧ H') |
| F' | ∃s > t, φ(s) | F ↔ (φ ∨ F') |
| P' | ∃s < t, φ(s) | P ↔ (φ ∨ P') |

### Conservative Extension Strategy

The extension would be **definitionally conservative**:
- Define: `G' φ := G φ ∧ ¬φ` (strict future excludes present)
- Or add G'/H' as primitives with axioms, then prove equivalence

### Research Questions

1. **Is reflexive completeness achievable?** The F/P gap suggests strict semantics may simplify the canonical model construction
2. **Density under strict semantics**: The density axiom (GGφ → Gφ) is documented for strict semantics
3. **Conservative extension proof**: Show formulas without G'/H' are derivable in extended system iff derivable in base
